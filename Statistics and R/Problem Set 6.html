Problem 1

A

The relationship between \( y_t \) and \( y_{t-1} \) is decreasing, while the relationship between \( y_t \) and \( y_{t-2} \) is increasing. This makes sense given the negative coefficient of the predictor. The current time step will be negative if the previous time step was positive, and vice versa. This alternating influence is just a portion of the impact that the previous positions have on the current one.

The visualization of the partial autocorrelation function supports modeling this data with an autoregressive model, and the extreme data drops off after a lag of 1.

B

The relationship between \( y_t \) and \( y_{t-1} \) is increasing, while the relationship between \( y_t \) and \( y_{t-2} \) appears to be random, with zero mean, suggesting no dependency.

The ACF and PACF suggest that the model belongs to an ARMA class series, with \( p > 5 \) and \( q = 2 \). \[ \hat{y}_t = \alpha\epsilon_{t-1} + \epsilon_t, \quad \alpha \in [0..1] \\ \] The likelihood of the predictor \( \alpha \) is inversely proportional to the sum of squared errors. The Maximum Likelihood Estimator is the value of the parameter \( \hat{\alpha} \) that minimizes the sum of squared errors. \[ \hat{\alpha}_{MLE} = \underset{\alpha}{\operatorname{argmin}} = \sum_{i = 0}^t (y_i - \hat{y}_i)^2 \\ \]
