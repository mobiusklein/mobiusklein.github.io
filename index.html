Who am I?

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Boston University's Program for Bioinformatics. I work on problems related to glycomics and glycoproteomics, primarily using mass spectrometry.

I am a formally trained computer scientist who got attached to artificial intelligence and programming languages but got caught up along the way with this funny business of debugging wetware. The majority of my real work is Python, with C thrown in for those problems which Python is unsuited for by still needs to do. Because people demand I draw pictures and give them buttons to press, I also use an unhealthy amount of JavaScript and related web technologies.
